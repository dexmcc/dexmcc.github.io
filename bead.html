Surface tension is an extremely evocative idea. A barrier around something seen as passable, it defies ideas of predictability and intuition. This basin, a type of vessel that exaggerates these features, holds speculative mappings - depicting what might happen if the ocean level rises even just a small amount. These are layered in with mirrorized maps generated from a small patch of moss; rendered as if they were the same scale as these geographical features. In combination with these maps, I used my frit technique on some layers to allow water to carry the green dots as it dried out and make some maps on its own. While this piece originally came from the proximity to the surface of the ocean I had at the time, it became something of an introspection. When anxiety rises, and the future remains as unpredictable as it once was, even as our methods of seeing within and collecting data grow stronger, things that are large become small, and things that seem insignificant become present everywhere. Surface tension, and its unpredictable mechanisms, becomes the cause and the relief.
