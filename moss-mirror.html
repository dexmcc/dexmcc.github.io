I found myself fascinated by a large patch of moss on a balcony I often look out from. From where I sat, the moss took up as much space of my view as the city behind it. While they are on the surface very different and of wildly different scales, both city and moss are ecosystems that contain life. By enlarging the pattern of the moss and recreating it using reflective surfaces, the moss can be read as a geographical map consisting of the metal surfaces found in cities.
