About me

I’m Dexter McChesney, a game maker and studio artist currently based in London. I Graduated from the Brown/Rhode Island School of Design Dual Degree program in 2024, studying Fine Arts - Glass, and Modern Culture and Media. I currently work as an independent artist, Game developer and assistant at multiple glass studios, and am developing my professional artistic practice. In general, I’m interested by themes of interactivity and narrative, and have always been fascinated by the ways we create stories to perceive the world. Through examining this, I learn more about how I see the world, and how I can create in ways that cause people to think about their perception. I am also an avid enjoyer of board games, movies, and Magic The Gathering.

Artist Statement

How big is the rain? Dexter McChesney has based a practice around finding questions like these and figuring out how we answer them. exploring the systems and narratives we create to comprehend the world around us, Dexter focuses on using glass’ boundary-defying nature as a way of delving into what separates man and nature. Dexter received a BFA in glass from the Rhode Island School of Design, and a BA in modern culture and media from Brown University.

Game Developer Bio

I am a game design generalist and developer, with a deep love of how various types of skills and people come together to make a game. I primarily have experience working in Unity and Godot, but I have experimented with other engines like Unreal 4. While I am primarily a designer and developer, I have also experienced production, writing, art, and sound, and have a strong ability to bring these different aspects together. Having been interested in game development and design before getting to university, I started my journey as a game maker as part of the Brown/RISD game development group. Over time, Game dev has become more and more a huge part of my life, and each project I make is more ambitious and complex than the last. I continue to develop my skills through jams and personal projects with friends while preparing to enter the field professionally.
